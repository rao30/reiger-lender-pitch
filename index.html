Legal Use - Residential Hotel / Lodging

Dallas Code Framework

These buildings are operated as long-term residential lodging (room-by-room leases, 30+ day stays) - the use commonly described as a residential hotel / lodging house - not as nightly short-term rentals and not as conventional apartment flats with full kitchens in every room.

What the use is

  • Private sleeping rooms rented separately on a monthly / 30+ day basis.
  • Shared baths and/or kitchens by building design - classic lodging / boarding / residential-hotel product.
  • Not Chapter 42B short-term rental lodging (stays under 30 consecutive days).
  • Stabilization thesis keeps that same legal use: renovate rooms in place, raise rents to ~$725/room, improve occupancy.

Why zoning fits

  • DCAD lists the sites as MFR - Multifamily District 2 (Dallas MF-2 family).
  • Under Dallas Development Code lodging uses, lodging / boarding house is permitted by right in MF-2 / MF-3 / MF-4 districts.
  • That is the code path for multi-room residential lodging on these lots - the portfolio does not require a commercial hotel strip or STR registration to operate as long-stay rooms.

How it stays compliant in practice

  • Certificate of occupancy aligned to the lodging / multi-tenant residential use for each building.
  • Multi-tenant registration with Dallas Code Compliance (Chapter 27) - lodging/boarding houses and extended-stay lodging are expressly in the multi-tenant inspection program.
  • Periodic city inspections / minimum housing standards - same compliance stack lenders already see on Dallas multi-tenant assets.
  • Post-gut renovations will be pulled under building permits and, where required, an updated CO so the improved product matches the permitted use.

What we tell underwriters

  • Collateral is four adjacent Munger Place buildings legally positioned for room-rental residential lodging.
  • Revenue is lease income from rooms (in-place carry during phased gut; then stabilized $725/room) - not ADR / hotel RevPAR underwriting.
  • Closing diligence confirms current CO language, multi-tenant certificates, and that seller registrations transfer or are reissued to the buyer entity.
  • Phased rehab does not change the use category - it upgrades the same residential-hotel product building by building.

Why This Works for a Lender

Controlled risk ramp. Only one building is offline for gut work at a time. As 5210 stabilizes, its NOI begins offsetting carry before 5204's larger rehab starts.

Transparent collateral advance. Draws track hard costs: purchase and closing at day zero, then equal monthly rehab draws against the active building's SF x $/SF budget.

Interest aligned with capital at risk. Eliminating Dutch interest reduces borrower burn and interest-reserve sizing - savings shown live versus a full-commitment accrual.

Clear exit to permanent debt. Take-out underwrites to stabilized NOI at a market cap, with LTV and DSCR gates you can stress in the controls.
